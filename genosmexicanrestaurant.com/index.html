Welcome to Geno's Mexican Restaurant, we are a family run Mexican Restaurant. We took over Soco's La Burrita in Phoenix, Oregon on May 1st, 2015. We have the same menu that has been around for years, not wanting to change what people love but at the same time we wanted to add some of our own touches to the cuisine. We make all our own salsas from scratch, from roasting the chili's to blending up all that spicy (and non spicy) goodness. Nothing we serve comes pre-made, we make our own corn tortillas, Chili Relleno, guacamole, beans, rice and much more. Our al pastor meat is some of the best around, seasoned just right it is perfect for tacos or a nice burrito. We have a vision of providing great service and great food as quick as possible.

Street Tacos

These tacos are perfect for a quick snack and are great for an alternative to a veggie or cheese platter for any party. Order different meats and set them out on a platter and you will have some happy guests.

We have changed what we put on the tacos, they now include any meat that we have for the other items including Carne Asada, Pollo Asado, Al Pastor and many many more.

We top our tacos with cilantro, onions and cabbage with your choice of different salsas.

New Salsas

We have several different homemade salsas for you to put on your taco, burrito or just use for some chip dip. They include a mild and a spicy tomatillo sauce, a mild and a spicy guacamole sauce and also a habanero sauce. We still have the good jalapeno salsa that has always been here but we felt there needed to be more choices so we just expanded the selection a bit.

Chimichangas

Come in and try one of our famous chimichangas, a staple here for years. A chimichanga is a deep fried burrito that is stuffed with meat, rice, and beans and topped with salsa, cheese, lettuce, pico de gallo, sour cream, and guacamole. You can enjoy a chimichanga with any of our meat choices or you can have a vegetarian chimichanga. We use Soybean oil to fry our food and we do not use lard in our refried beans. This makes for a truly vegetarian chimichanga.
